How to Choose a Licensed Money Lender in Singapore: The Complete 2026 Checklist

Finding yourself in need of financial assistance is a situation many Singaporeans face. Whether it is for an unexpected medical bill, urgent home repair, or bridging a temporary shortfall, choosing the right lender is critical.

This guide explains how to verify licensed moneylenders, identify scams, and make safe borrowing decisions.

Registry of Moneylenders Singapore

The official Registry of Moneylenders managed by the Ministry of Law is the only trusted source of legal lenders.

✔ Only lenders listed in the Registry are legal ✔ Around 153 licensed moneylenders (as of 2026)

How to Use the Registry

  • Visit Ministry of Law website
  • Download latest licensed lenders list
  • Search by registered business name
  • Verify licence number and address
  • Check official website authenticity

Verifying a Licensed Moneylender

Physical Office Check

  • Visit registered office
  • Check displayed licence certificate
  • Ensure professional setup

Document Handling

Licensed lenders must NOT keep your NRIC, passport, or ATM card.

Communication Rules

❌ No SMS, WhatsApp, or cold calls allowed

7 Key Criteria to Evaluate a Lender

1. Interest Rates

  • Max 4% monthly interest
  • Late fee: S$60 max
  • Admin fee: up to 10%
  • Total cost cannot exceed loan principal

2. Loan Contract

  • Loan amount
  • Interest rate
  • Repayment schedule
  • Total payable

3. Face-to-Face Requirement

Loan approval must be done physically in person.

4. Credit Check

Licensed lenders must perform credit checks.

5. Borrowing Limits

  • Below S$10,000 income: max S$3,000
  • Above S$20,000: up to 6× monthly income

6. Reviews & Reputation

Check Google reviews and independent platforms.

7. Physical Presence

A legitimate lender must have a registered office.

NBM Pilot Scheme

The New Business Model (NBM) Pilot Scheme ensures higher standards for lenders.

  • Minimum S$1 million capital
  • Secure Singpass integration
  • Ethical debt practices

Red Flags of Loan Scams

⚠ Unsolicited loan offers ⚠ Requests for Singpass credentials ⚠ Upfront payment before loan ⚠ No physical office ⚠ Pressure or threats

Licensed vs Illegal Lenders

Borrowing from loan sharks is illegal and dangerous. Always verify lenders through official channels.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I verify a lender online?

Yes, via the Ministry of Law Registry.

What if a lender is unlicensed?

Report to authorities or police hotline.

Required documents?

  • NRIC / Passport
  • Income proof

Can I borrow from multiple lenders?

Yes, within legal limits.

What if I cannot repay?

Contact lender early or seek financial counselling.

Conclusion

By verifying lenders, understanding your rights, and spotting scams, you can borrow safely and confidently in Singapore.
